Four years went by faster than I ever expected. I graduated from Rio Americano as valedictorian, earned my Seal of Biliteracy, and got to live a high school experience that I'm endlessly grateful for. I captained our water polo team to three section championships, played in Rio Band, taught myself guitar, led worship at Restoration Life Church, and am now employed building AI systems with local companies. I've tried to open every door I could, and each opportunity has changed me.

This August I'm heading to Baylor University to study Finance through the Business Scholars Program. I want to build a strong foundation in how money and organizations actually work, pair it with the technical skills I'm already building, and one day use all of it to steward resources well and grow stable companies. I have a feeling Waco is going to teach me things I can't even picture yet, and I'm ready for it.

None of this was mine to do alone, and I know it. Every step has had God's hand on it, and He's surrounded me with people who believed in me more than I believe in myself. To my parents, family, coaches, pastors, teachers, and friends who have showed up in the seasons that mattered most: thank you. Thank you for praying for me, for pushing me, and for being a part of my high school chapter. I genuinely would not be standing here without you.

So thank you for celebrating with me. I can't wait to see what God does with what comes next, and I hope I get to spend the rest of my life pouring back into people the way you all have poured into me.
